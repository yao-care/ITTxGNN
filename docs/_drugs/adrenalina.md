## Why Are There No Predictions?

Adrenalina (Epinephrine) is an endogenous catecholamine that acts on α- and β-adrenergic receptors, producing widespread sympathomimetic effects including bronchodilation, vasoconstriction, and cardiac stimulation. It is a cornerstone of emergency medicine globally.

However, the TxGNN model returned **zero predicted indications** for this compound. Several factors may explain this:

1. **Knowledge graph coverage**: The drug entry may be incomplete or absent in the knowledge graph used by TxGNN. The DrugBank ID was not resolved in the evidence pack, which could indicate a mapping failure between the INN "ADRENALINA" (the Italian/Latin form) and the standard entry for Epinephrine (DrugBank: DB00668).
2. **Broad existing indication profile**: Epinephrine already has a very broad set of approved indications (anaphylaxis, cardiac arrest, local anaesthesia adjunct, open-angle glaucoma, septic shock, croup, asthma). The model may not have identified additional novel indications beyond this already extensive list.
3. **Data gaps**: The evidence pack is missing the mechanism of action (MOA) field, original indications, and the DrugBank ID — all of which are critical inputs for generating meaningful predictions.
